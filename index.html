Chapter One - التصورات | Concepts

Table of Contents

1.1 Expressions indicate the meaning of what types of things?

An expression | اللفظ indicates the following meanings through assignment:

  1. The entire thing it is assigned to. This is an equal connection | مطابقة .
    • e.g. the expression "human" indicates Human (The Rational Animal).
  2. A part of the thing it is assigned to, if it has a part. This is a part within a whole connection | التضمن.
    • e.g. the expression "human" indicates Aisha bint Abu Bakr, may Allah have mercy on her, who is a part of "human[kind]".
  3. The thing it necessitates to be produced in the mind. This is a necessary connection | الإلتزام
    • e.g. the expression "human" indicates "something that can have the knowledge and capability of writing", because this concept necessitates one to think of a human being, as humans are the only creatures who know how to write

1.2 What are the types of expressions?

An expression | اللفظ has two categories:

  1. A singular expression | مفرد is an expression that refers to a singular meaning, not a part of a meaning.
    • e.g. the expression "human" refers to the singular meaning of "The Rational Animal". "Hu-" does not refer to rational, and "man" does not refer to animal, but rather all of the expression refers to a singular meaning.
  2. A compound expression |مؤلف is an expression where different parts of the expression refer to different parts of the meaning.
    • e.g. the expression "the rock's thrower", where rock indicates a rock, and thrower indicates the act of throwing, and together they refer to the person who throws a rock.

Singular expressions | المفرد are further divided into two categories:

  1. A universal expression | الكلي is an expression that CAN have a subset of expressions that share a meaning.
    • e.g. the universal expression "human" can have a subset of individual human beings like Aisha bint Abu Bakr, Umar ibn al-Khattab, Ali ibn Abu Talib, who all share the meaning of human.
  2. A particular expression | الجزئي is an expression that CANNOT have a subset of expressions that share a meaning.
    • e.g. the particular expression "Aisha bint Abu Bakr" cannot have a subset of anything else that shares its meaning.

Universal expressions | الكلي are further divided into two categories:

  1. An essential universal expression | الذاتي is an expression that connects to an essential quality of its subsets.
    • e.g. the essential universal expression "animal" connects to an essential quality of humans, horses, fish, etc., that are subsets of animal.
  2. A nonessential universal expression | العرضي is an expression that connects to a nonessential quality of its subsets.
    • e.g. the nonessential universal expression "beings that laugh" connects to a nonessential quality of human beings, which are a subset of beings that laugh

1.3 The Five Universals

Let's examine The Five Universals | الكليات الخمس . Remember that each universal will either fall under the category of being essential | الذاتي or accidental | العرضي.

  1. The genus | الجنس is something that has subsets of things that share qualities, when those subsets can also have a subset.
    • This universal falls under the essential | الذاتي category
    • e.g. the expression "animal" can have the subsets of humans, horses, fish, birds, etc, and humans, horses, fish, birds also have their own subsets.
  2. The species | النوع is something that is a subset of a genus | الجنس, i.e., it shares qualities with other species, and also has its own subsets.
    • This universal falls under the essential | الذاتي category.
    • e.g. humans, horses, fish, and birds, all share the qualities of being a heterotroph, a multicellular eukaryotic organism, etc. that make them a subset of the genus animal, and they also have their own subsets.
  3. The difference | الفصل is the essential quality that differentiates a species | النوع from all the other species within its genus | الجنس.
    • This universal falls under the essential | الذاتي category.
    • e.g. the quality of being rational is what differentiates humans from other animals.
  4. The property |الخاصة is a quality of a thing, that is not the essential quality of that thing, but is also not shared by other things.
    • This universal falls under the accidental | العرضي category, and can either be a inseperable accident | العرضي اللازم or a seperable accident | العرضي المفارق
    • e.g. the properties of "having the capability to laugh" and "actually laughing" are not the essential quality of being human, but also are only properties of humans. The former is an inseperable accident | العرضي اللازم, as all humans have the capability of laughing, while the latter is a seperable accident | العرضي المفارق, as not all humans actually laugh, or are laughing at every moment.
  5. The general accident | العرض العام is a quality of a thing, that is not the essential quality of that thing, and it is also shared by other things.
    • This universal falls under the accidental | العرضي category, and can either be a inseperable accident | العرضي اللازم or a seperable accident | العرضي المفارق
    • e.g. the general accidents "having the capability to breath" and "actually breathing" are not the essential qualities of being human, and are also shared by horses, elephants, etc. The former is an inseperable accident | العرضي اللازم, as all humans have the capability of breathing, while the latter is a seperable accident | العرضي المفارق, as not all humans are breathing at every moment.

2.1 Definitions & 2.2 Descriptions

A definition | الحد is a statement that indicates the essence of a thing. It has two types:

  1. The perfect definition | الحد التام is a definition comprised of the nearest genus | الجنس القريب and the difference | الفصل
    • e.g. the perfect defintion for human is "The rational animal".
  2. The imperfect definition | الحد الناقص is a definition comprised of a further genus |الجنس البعيد.
    • e.g. an imperfect definition of a human is "The rational organism".

A description |الرسم is a statement that describes a thing. It has two types:

  1. The perfect description | الرسم التام is a definition comprised of the nearest genus | الجنس القريب and a necessary property | الخاص اللازم .
    • e.g. the perfect description for human is "The laughing animal".
  2. The imperfect description | الرسم الناقص is a description comprised of multiple accidents |العرضيات, which together specify the thing it is describing.
    • e.g. an imperfect description of a human is "Something that walks upon two legs, has opposable thumbs, and walks in an upright manner".

Chapter Three - القضايا | Propositions

A proposition | القضية is a statement that can be verified or falsified.

Table of Contents

3.1 Types of Propositions In Regards to Whether Their Two Parts are Singular or Not

A proposition | القضية has two categories regarding whether the two parts of the proposition are comprised of singular statements | المفرد or not.

  1. The predicative proposition | الحملية is a propositon composed of singular statements | المفرد. Its two parts are called the subject | الموضوع and the predicate | المحمول .
    • e.g. the predicative proposition "Zayd is a writer" can be said to be true or false. "Zayd" is the subject and "writer" is the predicate.
  2. The conditional proposition |الشرطية is a proposition comprised of compound statements. Its two parts are called the antecedent | المقدمة and the consequence |التالي.

A conditional proposition | الشرطية can further be broken down into two categories:

  1. The conjuctive conditional proposition | الشرطية المتصلة follows an if-then pattern.
    • e.g. the conjuctive conditional proposition "If the sun rises, then it is day time" can be verified or falsified. "If the sun rises" is the antecedent | المقدمة and "then it is day time " is the consequence |التالي.
  2. The disjunctive proposition |الشرطية المنفصلة follows an either-or pattern.
    • e.g. the disjunctive proposition "In Yemen, it is either day or night" can be verified or falsified. "It is either day" is the antecedent | المقدمة and "or it is night " is the consequence |التالي.

3.2 Types of Propositions In Regards to Its Concordance to Reality Being Asserted or Negated

A proposition | القضية has two categories relating to how its concordance to reality | الحكم is being connected to reality

  1. A proposition with a positive connection | الموجبة affirms that something concords to reality, and this can either be verified or falsified.
    • e.g. the proposition "Zayd is a writer" asserts that Zayd is a writer.
  2. A proposition with a negative connection |السالبة denies that something concords to reality, and this can either be verified or falisfied.
    • e.g. the proposition "Zayd is not a writer" denies that Zayd is a writer.

3.3 Types of Propositions In Regards to Whether Its Concordance to Reality is For a Specific Thing or Not

A proposition | القضية has three categories relating to how its concordance to reality | الحكم relates to individuals or groups.

  1. A proposition with an individual connection | المخصوصة proposes that its predicate concords to reality for one individual thing.
    • e.g. the proposition "Zayd is a writer" asserts that Zayd, and only Zayd, is a writer.
  2. A proposition with a collective connection | المسورة proposes that its predicate concords to reality for a group of things.
    • e.g. the proposition "All human beings are writers" asserts that all of humanity are writers.
  3. A proposition with an indeterminate connection |المهملة proposes that its predicate concords to reality for an indeterminate thing.
    • e.g. for the proposition "The human is a writer", we do not know if we are asserting that one human is a writer, or that multiple humans are writers.

A proposition with a collective connection | المسورة can further be broken down into two categories:

  1. A proposition with a universal collective connection | كلية مسورة proposes that its predicate concords to reality for all of a group of things.
    • e.g. the proposition "All human beings are writers" asserts that all of humanity are writers.
  2. A proposition with a partial collective connection | جزئية مسورة proposes that its predicate concords to reality for some of a group of things.
    • e.g. the proposition "Some human beings are writers" asserts that only some members of humanity are writers.
